A research project by J-Six Team at Pravara Rural Engineering College, Loni

Understanding Digital Addiction

In today's hyper-connected world, students face unprecedented challenges with mobile and social media usage. Our research explores the impact of digital dependency on academic performance, mental health, and social relationships among engineering students.

Benefits of Digital Connectivity

  • Instant access to educational resources
  • Enhanced communication with peers and faculty
  • Opportunities for online learning and skill development
  • Networking with professionals in the field
  • Access to global knowledge repositories

Risks of Excessive Usage

  • Decreased attention span during lectures
  • Sleep deprivation from late-night usage
  • Increased anxiety and depression symptoms
  • Physical health issues (eye strain, poor posture)
  • Reduced face-to-face social interactions

Our Research Findings

Screen Time Patterns

Average engineering students spend 6-8 hours daily on mobile devices, with social media accounting for nearly 60% of usage during academic hours.

Productivity Impact

Students who practiced scheduled device usage showed 28% improvement in academic performance compared to peers with unrestricted access.

Behavioral Effects

Our surveys reveal that 74% of students experience 'phantom vibration syndrome', and 63% report feeling anxious when separated from their devices.
